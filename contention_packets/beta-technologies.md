# Invalidity Contention Packet — Beta Technologies patent estate

**Target:** Beta Technologies, Inc. and its asserted and pending patent claims around lift+cruise distributed-electric-propulsion eVTOL.

**Representative patent estate:**

- Beta Technologies patent estate (~30 issued US patents 2018–2025) covering lift+cruise eVTOL with mode-shutdown transition, BLDC propulsion, and Part 23 SVO certification basis.
- Beta's distinctive claim area is the lift+cruise architecture with stopped-and-feathered lift rotors in cruise plus a separate pusher cruise propeller (the Alia-250 / CX300 production design).

This packet maps the architectural claim domains of the Beta Technologies patent estate to corpus entries that anticipate each domain.

## Domain 1 — Lift+cruise architecture (separate lift rotors stopping in cruise + separate pusher)

**Beta claim area:** dedicated lift rotors that operate in hover, stop and feather in cruise, with separate cruise propulsion. The Alia-250's 4-lift + 1-pusher configuration.

**Anchor 102 prior art:**

- **1923** — `cierva-autogyro` — Foundational compound rotorcraft architecture: forward thrust separate from rotor lift; tractor propeller for cruise + autorotating top rotor. Cierva's Spanish patent ES81,006 (1922) and GB Patent 192,154 (1923) — long expired.
- **1943** — `doblhoff-wnf-342` — Compound rotorcraft architecture switching between hover (rotor powered by tip-burners) and cruise (rotor autorotates, forward thrust via tractor propeller). The mode-switching of lift propulsion between flight regimes.
- **1957** — `fairey-rotodyne` — Production-scale compound rotorcraft with tip-burner rotor + tractor propeller cruise. Set FAI compound-rotorcraft world speed record 1959. UK public-domain disclosure.
- **1959** — `kamov-ka-22-vintokryl` — Soviet compound: wingtip lift rotors + tractor propellers for cruise. Specifically anticipates the split-mode propulsion topology.
- **2014** — `nasa-gl-10` — Modern DEP tilt-wing with mode-switching of lift rotors in different flight regimes. NASA public-domain.
- **2016** — `nasa-x-57-maxwell` — DEP applied to fixed-wing with high-lift propellers active only in takeoff/landing — the architectural pattern of regime-specific rotor activation.
- **2018** — `wisk-cora` — Boeing/Wisk autonomous lift+cruise (12+1) with mode-shutdown transition.
- **2018** — `embraerx-evtol-concept` — Original 2018 EmbraerX 8+1 lift+cruise architecture (predates Beta Alia by 2 years).

**103 obviousness chain:** Cierva (1923) + Doblhoff (1943) + Fairey Rotodyne (1957) + NASA GL-10 (2014) + NASA X-57 (2016) + EmbraerX (2018) together disclose every architectural element Beta claims around lift+cruise mode-shutdown.

## Domain 2 — Distributed electric propulsion (DEP) on multi-rotor lift

**Beta claim area:** plurality of independently-controlled BLDC electric lift rotors with redundant electrical bus.

**Anchor 102 prior art:**

- **1886** — `verne-albatross` — Foundational anticipation. Distributed electric lift airscrews + horizontal cruise propellers decoupled from lift.
- **1922** — `de-bothezat-quadrotor` — First manned multi-rotor lift; differential-thrust attitude control across plural independent rotors.
- **1958** — `curtiss-wright-vz-7` — U.S. Army flying jeep: corner-mounted open-rotor quadrotor with cross-shafted single-engine drive. Mechanical analogue of modern DEP with shared DC bus.
- **2011** — `volocopter-vc1` — First manned electric BLDC multicopter.
- **2014** — `nasa-gl-10` — NASA's foundational DEP disclosure: 10 BLDC-driven rotors on tilt-wing, comprehensively published.
- **2016** — `aurora-lightningstrike-xv-24a` — DARPA VTOL X-Plane: 24 ducted lift fans embedded in wing and canard, turbine-electric hybrid. DARPA public-domain.

See Joby Domain 2 for the full DEP contention chain (same prior art base).

## Domain 3 — Coaxial lift-rotor pairs

**Beta claim area:** Beta Alia-250 uses paired lift rotors mounted on twin booms. Beta has filed multiple patents on the specific coaxial-pair lift geometry.

**Anchor 102 prior art:**

- **1922** — `pescara-helicopter` — Foundational coaxial counter-rotating rotor architecture with cyclic and collective pitch. Pescara's Spanish patent ES86,124 (1920) — long expired. Public domain for over a century.
- **1941** — `flettner-fl-282-kolibri` — Production intermeshing-rotor architecture (lateral coaxial-equivalent geometry).
- **1953** — `kaman-hh-43-huskie` — Production intermeshing-rotor (Kaman's expired US 2,496,857). Public domain.
- **1965** — `kamov-ka-26` — Soviet civil coaxial counter-rotating rotors. Public domain post-1991.
- **1991** — `kaman-k-max` — Modern certified intermeshing-rotor heavy-lift; mode-shutdown pattern (rotor stops, autorotates, restarts).
- **2003** — `leonardo-aw609` — Civil tilt-rotor with coaxial-pair lift heritage.

**103 obviousness chain:** Pescara (1922) + Kaman HH-43 (1953) + Kamov Ka-26 (1965) together comprehensively place coaxial lift-rotor architectural claims in the public domain.
